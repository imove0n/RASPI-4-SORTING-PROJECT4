========================================================
STEP 1: PHOTOGRAPHING TOMATOES FOR YOLO TRAINING
========================================================

GOAL: Take 60-100 photos of tomatoes in different conditions

TIME NEEDED: 20-30 minutes
EQUIPMENT: Phone camera or any camera


WHAT YOU NEED:
==============
- 10-15 tomatoes (mix of ripe, unripe, rotten)
- Good lighting (natural light or room light)
- Different backgrounds (table, cloth, paper, etc.)
- Your phone camera


PHOTO REQUIREMENTS:
===================

Quantity:
---------
MINIMUM: 60 photos total
RECOMMENDED: 100+ photos

Categories:
-----------
- 30-40 photos of RIPE tomatoes (red, orange, dark red)
- 30-40 photos of UNRIPE tomatoes (green, light green)
- 20+ photos of ROTTEN tomatoes (spots, damage, worm bites)


HOW TO TAKE PHOTOS:
===================

For EACH Tomato (10-20 shots per tomato):
------------------------------------------

1. DIFFERENT ANGLES:
   - Top view
   - Side view (left)
   - Side view (right)
   - Bottom view
   - 45-degree angle

2. DIFFERENT DISTANCES:
   - Close-up (tomato fills frame)
   - Medium (tomato + some background)
   - Far (tomato is smaller in frame)

3. DIFFERENT ROTATIONS:
   - Front
   - Rotate 90 degrees → photo
   - Rotate 90 degrees → photo
   - Rotate 90 degrees → photo

4. DIFFERENT BACKGROUNDS:
   - On white paper
   - On dark table
   - On cloth
   - On wood surface
   - On your conveyor belt (if you have it)

5. DIFFERENT LIGHTING:
   - Natural window light
   - Room light overhead
   - Side lighting
   - Slightly darker (not too dark!)


IMPORTANT TIPS:
===============

✓ DO:
-----
- Keep tomato in focus (not blurry)
- Include some background objects (books, cups, etc.)
- Take photos with other tomatoes in background
- Vary the environment
- Show damaged parts clearly (for rotten category)
- Make sure tomato is visible and clear

✗ DON'T:
--------
- Don't make photos too dark
- Don't blur the tomato
- Don't have tomato too small in frame
- Don't take all photos from same angle
- Don't use only one background


EXAMPLE SESSION (One Ripe Tomato):
===================================

1. Place tomato on white paper → photo (top view)
2. Rotate tomato 90° → photo
3. Rotate tomato 90° → photo
4. Rotate tomato 90° → photo
5. Pick up tomato, show from side → photo
6. Move camera closer → photo (close-up)
7. Move camera farther → photo
8. Place tomato on dark table → photo (change background)
9. Rotate tomato → photo
10. Tilt camera 45° → photo (different angle)
11. Place book next to tomato → photo (with distractor)
12. Add another tomato in background → photo
13. Change lighting → photo
14. Show other side → photo
15. Final rotation → photo

DONE! 15 photos of one tomato ✓

Repeat for all tomatoes!


ORGANIZING YOUR PHOTOS:
========================

Create folders on your computer:

tomato_photos/
├── all_photos/
    ├── IMG_001.jpg
    ├── IMG_002.jpg
    ├── IMG_003.jpg
    └── ... (all 60-100 photos here)

DON'T sort them into ripe/unripe/rotten yet!
We'll label them in Label Studio!


QUALITY CHECKLIST:
==================

Before moving to labeling, verify:

□ You have at least 60 photos total
□ Mix of ripe, unripe, and rotten tomatoes
□ Different angles for each tomato
□ Different backgrounds visible
□ Photos are clear and in focus
□ Good lighting (can see tomato clearly)
□ Some photos have multiple tomatoes
□ Some photos have other objects nearby


SAMPLE BREAKDOWN:
=================

If you have 10 tomatoes:
- 4 ripe tomatoes × 10 photos each = 40 photos
- 4 unripe tomatoes × 10 photos each = 40 photos
- 2 rotten tomatoes × 10 photos each = 20 photos

TOTAL: 100 photos ✓ (Excellent!)


WHAT'S NEXT:
============

After you finish taking photos:

1. Copy all photos to one folder
2. Upload to Google Drive (or prepare to upload)
3. Move to STEP 2: Labeling in Label Studio


READY TO START?
===============

1. Gather your tomatoes
2. Set up good lighting
3. Prepare different backgrounds
4. Start photographing!

Take your time - quality photos = better detection!

Good luck! 📸🍅
